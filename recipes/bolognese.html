Bolognese

Back to Index Bolognese sauce, or ragù alla bolognese, is a traditional Italian meat-based sauce originating from Bologna. It is typically served with pasta, particularly tagliatelle, and is known for its rich flavor and hearty texture.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 carrots, chopped
  • 2 celery stalks, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (28 ounces) crushed tomatoes
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 cup milk
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Fresh basil for garnish (optional)

Instructions

  1. In a large skillet, heat some olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onion, carrots, and celery, and sauté until softened.
  2. Add the minced garlic and ground beef, cooking until the meat is browned.
  3. Pour in the red wine and let it simmer for a few minutes.
  4. Add the crushed tomatoes and season with salt and pepper. Let it simmer for about 30 minutes.
  5. Stir in the milk and let it cook for an additional 10-15 minutes.
  6. Serve over cooked pasta and garnish with fresh basil if desired.

Notes

Bolognese sauce is best when made ahead of time, as the flavors develop and deepen with time. It can be stored in the refrigerator for up to 3 days or frozen for longer storage.

Serving Suggestions

Serve Bolognese sauce over tagliatelle or pappardelle pasta. It can also be used in lasagna or as a filling for stuffed pasta like ravioli.
